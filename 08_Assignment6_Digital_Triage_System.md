# Final Week 0 Deliverable – Digital Emergency Triage System

A digital emergency triage system is designed to assist healthcare professionals in identifying patients who may require urgent medical attention. The system works by collecting important patient information such as age, Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS), blood pressure, pulse rate, temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen requirements, and presenting symptoms. By analyzing this information, the system can quickly highlight abnormal findings and help prioritize patients according to their level of risk. The purpose of the system is not to replace healthcare professionals, but to support clinical decision-making and improve the efficiency of the triage process.

The first step in the digital triage process is data collection and validation. Patient information is entered into the system, and checks are performed to identify missing or invalid values. If important information is unavailable, the system flags the record for nurse review while continuing to use the available data. This helps maintain patient safety and ensures that incomplete records do not result in inappropriate triage decisions.

After the patient information has been collected, the system evaluates several important clinical indicators. Measurements such as GCS, blood pressure, pulse rate, respiratory rate, temperature, and FiO₂ are assessed because abnormal values may indicate clinical deterioration. The system assigns points to abnormal findings based on their potential severity. For example, a very low GCS score, low blood pressure, or high oxygen requirement may indicate a critically ill patient and therefore contribute more heavily to the overall risk score.

The system also evaluates the presence of critical warning signs such as chest pain, severe shortness of breath, major bleeding, stroke symptoms, unconsciousness, or severe trauma. These conditions often require immediate medical attention and are therefore given the highest priority within the scoring process. By combining vital sign abnormalities with critical clinical symptoms, the system can develop a more complete picture of the patient's condition rather than relying on a single measurement.

Once all assessments have been completed, the total risk score is used to categorize the patient into a risk level. Patients with the highest scores are classified as **High Risk / Immediate Attention**, indicating that urgent medical assessment is required. Patients with moderate scores are classified as **Moderate Risk / Urgent Review**, while patients with lower scores are classified as **Low Risk / Routine Assessment**. The system then displays the patient's risk category, abnormal findings, suggested urgency level, confidence level, and recommendations for clinical review.

One of the main advantages of this approach is that it considers multiple factors simultaneously. A patient may not appear critically ill based on a single measurement, but a combination of moderately abnormal findings may indicate a serious condition. The scoring system allows the digital triage model to evaluate the overall clinical picture and support more consistent decision-making. It also provides transparency by clearly identifying which findings contributed to the final risk classification.

However, the system should never be used as a replacement for professional clinical judgment. Healthcare professionals must always review the patient's condition, symptoms, and medical history before making final decisions. The digital triage system is intended to function as a decision-support tool that helps clinicians identify high-risk patients more quickly, improve patient prioritization, and support safer and more efficient emergency department operations.
